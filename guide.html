The Basics

Rails Girls events are fully non-profit. We don’t charge the participants and do not pay for coaches or speakers. Participants don’t need any previous knowledge about programming and there is no age-limitation. All they need is a laptop and some curiosity.

The two-day event includes a lot of small group working and short focused talks on programming, design and web. No panel discussions or podium-talks - the spirit should be informal and hands-on. The more you can remove abstractions and add inclusivity the better.

Boksiin: ails Girls philosophy Show sparks & keep in mind the big picture. Explain, repeat and always tie what your telling into a larger context. Internet was made by people, and it doesn’t break by a little tinkering. Continuosly show the human side: encourage coaches to talk about Copy-pasting rules. First event, held in November 26-27th, 2010 got over 100 interested girls signed-up for the workshop.

In 12 hours, 24 girls published 4 web applications.

Rails Girls aims to open up technology and make it more approachable for girls and women.

The weekend event is free and open to all enthusiastic girls and women. Rails for Girls wants to empower girls to build the capacity and acquire the tools to conquer the last online frontier. We are a fully non-profit operation with a small base-funding from Finnish Technology Industries Federation.
